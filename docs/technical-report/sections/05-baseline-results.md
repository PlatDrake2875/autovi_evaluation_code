# 5. Baseline Results

## 5.1 Centralized Training

We first establish a centralized baseline by training PatchCore on the complete aggregated training set (1,523 images across all 6 categories). This represents the upper bound performance achievable without privacy constraints.

### Training Configuration

- All training images pooled together
- Separate memory bank per object category
- Coreset selection: 10% of patches per category
- Total training time: ~15 minutes on single GPU

## 5.3 Analysis

### Category-wise Performance

- **Best**: engine_wiring (highest training samples, well-defined defects)
- **Worst**: underbody_screw (class imbalance: only 18 anomalous test images)

### Defect Type Analysis

| Defect Type | Mean AUC-sPRO@0.05 | Mean AUC-ROC |
|-------------|-------------------|--------------|
| Structural anomalies | 0.80 | 0.90 |
| Logical anomalies | 0.76 | 0.86 |

Structural anomalies (physical damage) are generally easier to detect than logical anomalies (missing components), which require understanding spatial relationships.

## 5.5 Comparison with Literature

Our centralized baseline performance aligns with reported PatchCore results on similar industrial datasets (MVTec AD), validating our implementation. The slight performance variations reflect the unique challenges of the AutoVI dataset:

- Real production conditions (vs. controlled lab settings)
- Higher image variability
- Mix of structural and logical defects
